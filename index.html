Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper (1906-1992) was a United States Navy rear admiral, mathematician, and computer scientist. She is a pioneer of computer programming and was the first to devise the theory of machine-independent programming languages. She used that theory to develop the FLOW-MATIC and COBOL programming languages.

  • Created the first programming language compiler.
  • Introduced the theory of machine-independent programming that led to the creation of programming languages such as COBOL.
  • Was one of the first female officers to achieve the real admiral rank in the U.S Navy.
  • Recieved the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2016 (posthumously).
